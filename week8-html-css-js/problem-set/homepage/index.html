La Traviata

Opera by Giuseppe Verdi

Home Trivia Synopsis Characters

La traviata by Verdi is a tragic opera that tells the story of Violetta, a beautiful and wealthy courtesan who falls in love with Alfredo, a young and noble man. Their romance is threatened by the social norms and prejudices of their time, as well as by Violetta’s illness of tuberculosis. The opera is based on a play by Alexandre Dumas fils, which was inspired by his own affair with a courtesan named Marie Duplessis. The opera was first performed in Venice in 1853, but it was not well received until it was revised and restaged in 1854. La traviata is one of the most popular and frequently performed operas in the world, and it contains some of Verdi’s most memorable melodies, such as “Sempre libera”, “Un dì felice”, and “Addio del passato”.
