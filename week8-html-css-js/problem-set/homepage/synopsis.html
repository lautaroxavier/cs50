Giuseppe Verdi

Verdi was a famous Italian composer of opera in the 19th century. He wrote some of the most popular and performed operas in the world, such as La traviata, Aida, and Otello. He was also a patriot who supported the unification of Italy and became a symbol of the Risorgimento movement. He was born in a small village near Busseto in 1813 and received his musical education with the help of a local patron. He married Margherita Barezzi, the daughter of his patron, in 1836, but lost both her and their two children by 1840. He then devoted himself to composing operas, chieving his first great success with Nabucco in 1842. He continued to write operas for the next 50 years, creating masterpieces that explored themes such as love, betrayal, revenge, freedom, and justice. He also composed a Requiem Mass in memory of the poet Alessandro Manzoni and four sacred pieces for choir and orchestra. He died in Milan in 1901 at the age of 87.

La traviata is divided into three acts, each with several scenes. The first act takes place in a Parisian salon, where Violetta, a courtesan, is hosting a party. She meets Alfredo, a young man who has been secretly in love with her for a year. He confesses his feelings to her and she is touched by his sincerity. She gives him a camellia and tells him to return when it wilts. After the guests leave, Violetta reflects on her life of pleasure and freedom, but also feels a new attraction to Alfredo.

The second act is set in a country house near Paris, where Violetta and Alfredo have been living happily for several months. Alfredo learns from his friend Gastone that Violetta has been selling her jewels to pay for their expenses. He decides to go to Paris to settle their debts. Meanwhile, Violetta receives an unexpected visit from Alfredo’s father, Giorgio Germont, who asks her to leave Alfredo for the sake of his family’s honor and reputation. He tells her that Alfredo’s sister’s marriage is in jeopardy because of their scandalous relationship. Violetta is torn between her love and her sacrifice, but eventually agrees to Germont’s request. She writes a farewell letter to Alfredo and leaves. Alfredo returns and finds the letter. He is devastated and vows revenge.

The third act takes place in Violetta’s bedroom in Paris, where she is dying of tuberculosis. She receives a letter from Germont, who informs her that he has told Alfredo the truth and that he is coming to ask for her forgiveness. She is overjoyed, but also realizes that it is too late for her. Alfredo arrives and embraces her. They dream of leaving Paris and starting a new life together. However, Violetta’s condition worsens and she collapses in his arms. Germont arrives with a doctor and witnesses the tragic scene. Violetta dies, asking God for mercy.
